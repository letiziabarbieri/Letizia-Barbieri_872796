Remikks

This lab provided a methodological
research path in product design problem
finding, setting and solving with analysis,
goal setting and concept generation.
This approach allowed us to acquire the
knowledge and tools for the metaproject
process. The focus of my group concerned
anonymity as a social, personal and
job-related matter. We then designed a
system called Remikks which found its
practical depiction in an app of basic
features. Remikks was a mobile app which
allowed people to attend events closely
related to their tastes in music genre
though in a complete and anonymous way.

Jack il signore dei fagioli

The Graphics Computer Laboratory interacted
with Architecture Information, an inter-disciplinary
field straddling the worlds of design, graphics
and publishing with a special focus on the
third dimension. The applied computer science
module dealt with web communication, revolving
around the issues of presentation of Computer
Graphics contents and design and implementation
of interactive elements. The Digital Design
module tackled the switch from bi-dimensional
representation to the creation of
three-dimensional models. The project of my
group was called Jack il Signore dei Fagioli
(literally Jack the Lord of the Beans)
and was an reinterpretation of Jack and the
Beanstalk through a Lord of the Rings lens.
We created three-dimensional models and
landscapes, coded games and made an
interactive webpage.

Spazio Grafico

The Lab was aimed at producing, at the
end of the educational program, an oriented
branding design project. It was oriented to the
acquisition of basic knowledge about the concepts
of brand, logotype, coordinated image and whatever
else is useful for creating graphic design
artifacts and their derivatives with a view to
constructing the brand identity. The project of
my group, Spazio Grafico, consisted in a graphic
museum characterized by an engaging, colloquial
and down to earth tone of voice, aimed at showing
the historic, social and economic background in
which a certain piece of graphic art was developed
rather than exposing it on a blank wall without
any kind of emotional engagement.
